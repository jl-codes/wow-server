The LAN

A 15-story tower on Market St, San Francisco. Hundreds of players. One raid. Every weekend. This is what gaming was supposed to be.

// the venue

Market St Tower, SF

15 floors. Dedicated raid floors, PvP arenas, social lounges, and a command center. The kind of space that makes you forget the outside world exists.

// the raid

Weekly PvE Raids

Every weekend, the community descends on the tower for the weekly raid. Clear bosses, earn loot, and get paid based on your Raid Output Score — weighted by class and role.

// the pvp

Live PvP Tournaments

Arena brackets and battleground competitions with live spectating. Weekly, monthly, and quarterly prize pools. The quarterly championship is the Super Bowl of this community.

2000s LAN Renaissance

sys.rules

The Code

Simple rules. No exceptions.

  • One person, one account. No alts. No sharing. Your seat is yours alone.
  • Accounts are transferable. You can sell your seat to another player at any agreed price.
  • $10/month subscription. Keeps the lights on and the prize pools funded.
  • LAN attendance is optional but that's where the real community lives.
  • No botting, no exploits. Compete with skill or don't compete at all.
  • Invite only. You need a link from an existing member or approval from the admins.
  • Max 100 seats. Once they're gone, the only way in is buying someone's account.

01

Download WoW 3.3.5a

ChromieCraft · Warmane

02

Set the Realmlist

Open Data/enUS/realmlist.wtf and replace with:

set realmlist 127.0.0.1

Use Your Credentials

You'll receive your account credentials after approval. Run Wow.exe (not the Launcher) and log in.
